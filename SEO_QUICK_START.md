### The Problem:
Your titles and descriptions look generic in Google Search Results. When people see 100 different "free QR generators" in the search results, they pick the one that **stands out**.

### The Solution (Examples):

**BEFORE (Generic):**
```
Title: "Free QR Code Generator - Create Custom QR Codes (No Signup) | PixTool"
Description: "The #1 free QR code generator for URLs, WiFi, vCards & text..."
```

**AFTER (Compelling):**
```
Title: "Free QR Code Generator 2026 - Create Custom Codes Instantly | PixTool"
Description: "🎯 Generate professional QR codes in 2 seconds. Custom colors, WiFi QR, vCards. Download 400x400px PNG. No signup. Zero-data privacy guarantee."
```

**Why This Gets More Clicks:**
- ✅ Adds freshness signal ("2026")
- ✅ Emoji makes it stand out (🎯)
- ✅ Specific benefits ("2 seconds", "400x400px")
- ✅ Trust signals ("Zero-data privacy")

4. Should see JSON-LD code
5. Copy to: https://validator.schema.org/

1. Google Search Console → Performance
2. Filter by specific page or query
3. Export data to track over time
